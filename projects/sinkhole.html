DNS Sinkhole

This project involved deploying Pi-hole on a Raspberry Pi 3 to act as a DNS sinkhole for network-level ad and telemetry blocking. I configured the Pi-hole to operate as the default DNS resolver for all devices on the home network, intercepting and rejecting DNS queries to known malicious or tracking domains.

The setup process included configuring static IP assignments, forwarding DNS queries through DNS-over-HTTPS using Cloudflare, and customizing blocklists for privacy and threat intelligence. I also implemented logging and dashboard access for real-time monitoring of DNS activity, allowing me to observe telemetry behavior across a range of devices including PCs, phones, smart TVs, and IoT hardware.

This project helped reinforce practical understanding of DNS traffic, passive network defense, and device fingerprinting through DNS metadata. It also served as a launching point for experimenting with custom firewall rules and future plans for recursive DNS configurations or blackhole routing.
